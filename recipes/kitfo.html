Description

Kitfo is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from finely minced raw beef, seasoned with a special blend of spices called mitmita and clarified butter (niter kibbeh). The meat is typically served lightly warmed, though it can also be fully cooked according to personal preference. Kitfo is known for its rich, buttery flavor and delicate texture, making it one of the most celebrated dishes in Ethiopian cuisine. It is often enjoyed on special occasions and is a staple in many Ethiopian households.

This dish is traditionally served with gomen (spiced collard greens) and ayib (a mild Ethiopian cheese) on the side, along with injera, a soft, sourdough flatbread that acts as both a plate and utensil. Some variations may also include kochko (spiced clarified butter) or a fresh salad to complement the rich flavors of the kitfo. Together, these sides balance the spiciness and richness of the dish, creating a harmonious and authentic Ethiopian dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 500g minced beef
  • 2 tbsp mitmita (spice blend)
  • 3 tbsp niter kibbeh (clarified butter)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Served with gomen, ayib, and injera

Steps

  1. Lightly warm the minced beef in a pan over low heat (optional: leave it raw if preferred).
  2. Add mitmita and niter kibbeh to the beef and mix thoroughly.
  3. Season with salt to taste and cook for a few minutes if warming.
  4. Serve the kitfo on a plate with injera as the base.
  5. Arrange the sides: gomen (collard greens) and ayib (Ethiopian cheese) alongside the meat.
  6. Optionally, add kochko or a fresh salad for extra flavor.
  7. Enjoy your traditional Ethiopian Kitfo!
